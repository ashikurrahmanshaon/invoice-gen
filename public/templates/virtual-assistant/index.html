Professional Invoicing Tips for Virtual Assistants

Ensure rapid payouts from your clients by following these standard administrative parameters:

  • Provide Task Breakdowns: Instead of writing "Admin Support", list categories (e.g., "Inbox Clean & Calendar Triage - 8 Hours").
  • Track Tool Subscriptions: If the client requires you to purchase software licenses, charge them as separate reimbursable items.
  • List Retainer vs Overages: If on a monthly hours bundle (e.g. 20 hours/mo), clearly indicate when hours cross into hourly overages.

Common Mistakes in VA Invoicing

Protect your cash flow against administrative delays by avoiding these three pitfalls:

1. Bundling Varied Services

Inbox cleanups, bookkeeping, and graphics creation require different effort levels. Itemize them separately to maintain high value tracking.

2. Unspecified Reporting Intervals

Corporate clients often require time logs. State the exact time intervals the invoice covers (e.g. "VA Services: July 1 - July 15").

3. Neglecting Secure Formats

Recommended Payment Terms for Virtual Assistants

VA business cash flows standardly run on these timelines:

  • Weekly/Bi-weekly billing: Best for ongoing, high-hour operations to maintain low outstanding liabilities.
  • Prepaid Hours retainers: Bill and collect 100% of the monthly hour package before work begins.
