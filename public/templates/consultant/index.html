Consultant Billing Structures

Unlike simple project tasks, professional consulting engagements usually require structured billing intervals. Choose the model that matches your agreement:

  • Advisory Retainers: List a monthly recurring flat fee (e.g. "Advisory Retainer Fee for July"). These are traditionally invoiced at the beginning of the service cycle.
  • Hourly Consulting: Detail the specific dates and strategy blocks consulting was rendered (e.g. "Strategy Planning Workshop - 4 hours").
  • Value-Based Project Fee: Best for targeted milestones where the price is determined by the output value rather than input hours.

Common Consulting Invoicing Pitfalls

Professional credibility is easily damaged by invoicing details. Avoid these common consulting mistakes:

1. Failing to Attach Timesheets

If you bill hourly, corporate clients often reject invoices that do not provide detailed descriptions of strategies explored. Itemize hours precisely.

2. Unclear Expense Reimbursement

If billing for travel or material expenses, clearly separate these as distinct line items and upload receipts separately to the client's accounting portal.

3. Poor Layout Quality

Corporate accounting departments process hundreds of invoices weekly. Having a cluttered, poorly aligned sheet signals a lack of executive polish.

Payment Terms for Corporate Consulting

Corporate procurement departments usually enforce rigid net-payment cycles. We recommend standardizing on these terms:

  • Net 30: The standard cycle for corporate accounts, requiring payment within 30 calendar days of invoice receipt.
  • Net 10 (with Early Discount): Offer a small percentage discount (e.g. 2% 10 Net 30) if they settle their balance within 10 days to improve your cash flow.
