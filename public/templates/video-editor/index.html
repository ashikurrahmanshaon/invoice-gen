Video Editing Invoicing Best Practices

Protect your creative labor by structuring your post-production invoices according to these guidelines:

  • Specify Revision Boundaries: Clearly list how many feedback cycles are included (e.g. "Video Edit (Includes 2 Revision Rounds)").
  • Itemize Stock Media & Asset Costs: If you purchased background audio licenses or stock footages, list them as distinct reimbursable lines.
  • Add Project Length/Output Details: Detail target lengths (e.g. "60-Second Promotional Social Video") to define the project scope.

Common Post-Production Invoicing Mistakes

Ensure smooth account processing by avoiding these three critical editor billing pitfalls:

1. Releasing Final Render Files Prior to Settlement

Always deliver watermarked or lower-res preview files first. Do not share full ProRes master files until the client has paid the Balance Due.

2. Allowing Scope Creep to Pass Unbilled

If the client requests additional cuts or script adjustments outside of the initial agreement, charge them as "Overage Revisions" lines.

3. Poor Date Tracking

Always state the exact timeline the edit took place to allow corporate marketing accounting teams to approve payments rapidly.
