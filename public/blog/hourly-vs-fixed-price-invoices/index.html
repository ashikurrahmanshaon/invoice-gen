Hourly vs Fixed Price Invoices: Which is Best?

Deciding whether to charge hourly or offer a fixed project price is a key business decision. The way you price your services shapes your invoicing structure. Both models have clear operational advantages and disadvantages.

1. The Hourly Billing Model

Hourly billing is ideal for projects with undefined scope. You invoice for the exact hours worked. Features include:

  • Pros: Guarantees payment for all hours worked. Prevents scope creep losses.
  • Cons: Limits your earning capacity based on physical hours. Requires meticulous time tracking.
  • Invoice Structure: List dates, total hours logged, and your hourly rate clearly (e.g. "React development - 8 hours @ $70/hr").

2. The Fixed Price Model

Fixed pricing works best when the project deliverables are clearly defined. You charge a set fee for the output, regardless of hours. Features include:

  • Pros: Rewards efficiency. Allows you to scale revenue as your speed increases.
  • Cons: Risk of unpaid hours if scope creep occurs without adjustments.
  • Invoice Structure: List specific project milestones (e.g. "Milestone 1: Branding Deliverables Complete").

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I handle scope creep in fixed-price projects?

Address changes immediately. File a separate estimate for "Additional Work Out of SOW", get client approval, and invoice it as a distinct line item.

Do hourly invoices require timesheet attachments?

Generally, yes. Corporate accounts departments often require detailed time logs to verify work before approving payouts.
