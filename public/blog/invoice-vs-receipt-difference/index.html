Invoice vs Receipt: What's the Difference?

Many independent contractors and business owners use the terms "invoice" and "receipt" interchangeably. However, they serve completely different purposes in the transaction cycle. Understanding these differences is crucial for accurate accounting and tax filings.

1. The Invoice: Requesting Payment

An invoice is issued before the payment is completed. It acts as an official request for payment and sets the transactional terms. Key characteristics of an invoice include:

  • Timing: Sent to the client once a milestone is completed or services are rendered, but prior to receiving funds.
  • Contents: Outlines payment terms (e.g. Net 15), payment methods, and an itemized breakdown of services.
  • Purpose: Used by both parties to record accounts receivable (for you) and accounts payable (for the client).

2. The Receipt: Confirming Payment

A receipt is issued after the payment is completed. It acts as proof that the transaction occurred. Key characteristics of a receipt include:

  • Timing: Sent immediately after funds are received.
  • Contents: Shows the amount paid, payment method used, transaction date, and the remaining balance (which should be zero).
  • Purpose: Serves as proof of purchase for tax deductions and audit protection.

How They Work Together

In a standard business cycle, the invoice comes first. It defines the payment request. Once your client processes the payment and you receive the funds, you send them a receipt. Maintaining both documents is essential for tax compliance and audit protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is an invoice a legal contract?

No. While an invoice is a legally binding request for payment under commercial agreements, it should be supported by a signed contract or statement of work.

Can I turn an invoice into a receipt?

Yes. You can update the invoice status to "Paid", record the date of payment, and send the updated document to serve as a receipt.
