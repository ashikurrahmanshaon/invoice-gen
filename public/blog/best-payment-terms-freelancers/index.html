Best Payment Terms for Freelancers

For independent freelancers, managing your billing cycle is critical to business survival. Choosing the wrong payment terms can lock up your working capital for months. Standardizing on strategic terms is the key to maintaining a steady cash flow.

1. Net 15: The Freelancer Standard

Net 15 means your client has 15 calendar days from the invoice date to complete the payment. This is the recommended default for freelance projects. It provides a tight timeline that prevents long payment cycles.

2. Net 30: The Corporate Default

Net 30 gives your client 30 calendar days to complete the payment. This is the default requirement for larger corporate accounting departments. While common, freelancers should negotiate Net 15 where possible to prevent cash flow dry spells.

3. 50% Upfront Retainer

For projects over $1,000, you should require a 50% upfront retainer before beginning work. This covers your initial labor hours and commits the client to the project. The final 50% is due upon completion or project exit.

To incentivize rapid payouts, you can offer a small percentage discount (e.g. 2% 10 Net 30), which gives the client a 2% discount if they settle their balance within 10 days.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I enforce payment deadlines?

Specify the terms clearly on the invoice. If a payment is missed, follow up immediately with a polite reminder email reference to prevent further delays.

Can I charge late fees on overdue invoices?

Yes. If late fees were outlined in your initial project contract, you can legally add a monthly interest surcharge to the outstanding balance.
